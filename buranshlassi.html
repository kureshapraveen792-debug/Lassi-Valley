The Traditional Indian Method

Our Buransh Lassi is prepared using a time-honored recipe that captures the essence of this Himalayan flower. While the exact proportions are a secret, the traditional method involves these steps:

  1. Flower Harvest: The vibrant red Buransh flowers are hand-picked from the high-altitude forests of the Himalayas during their peak blooming season.
  2. Extract Preparation: The petals are carefully separated and a fresh juice or squash is prepared by boiling them with a small amount of water and natural sweeteners.
  3. The Lassi Base: A thick, creamy, and fresh curd (dahi) is churned using a traditional wooden churner (madani) until it becomes smooth and frothy.
  4. Blending the Flavors: The freshly made Buransh extract is blended with the yogurt, along with a hint of cardamom and a touch of roasted cumin, creating a unique sweet and savory profile.
  5. Chilled to Perfection: The lassi is served chilled, offering a refreshing and cooling experience that is both healthy and delicious.
