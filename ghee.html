The Golden Elixir: Desi Ghee

Desi Ghee is more than just a cooking oil; it's a staple of traditional Indian cuisine and a powerhouse of health benefits. Made from pure butter, it's a sacred ingredient cherished for its rich aroma, nutty flavor, and nourishing properties.

Positive Impact & Health Benefits

  • Rich in Nutrients: A great source of fat-soluble vitamins like A, D, E, and K, which are crucial for immune function, bone health, and skin.
  • Aids Digestion: Desi Ghee helps in improving digestion by stimulating the secretion of stomach acids, making food easier to process.
  • Boosts Immunity: With its antioxidant properties, it helps in strengthening the body's defense mechanism against infections.
  • Heart-Friendly: Contains a healthy balance of fatty acids, including Omega-3s, which can help lower bad cholesterol levels.
  • Enhances Flavor: Its distinct, rich flavor adds depth to any dish, from curries and dals to sweets and rice.

Traditional Method: The Vedic Way

At Lassi Valley, we follow the time-honored, traditional method to ensure our Desi Ghee is of the highest purity and quality.

  1. Sourcing Pure Milk

    We begin with milk from happy, healthy cows, ensuring the highest quality from the very start. The milk is then boiled and cooled.

  2. Making Curd

    A small amount of curd (dahi) is added to the milk to set it overnight. This process is crucial as it creates a base that is rich in probiotics and nutrients.

  3. Churning the Curd

    The curd is then churned using a wooden churner in a traditional manner to separate the fresh butter (makkhan) from the buttermilk (chaas).

  4. Heating the Butter

    The fresh butter is slowly heated over a low flame. As it melts, the milk solids are separated, and the water evaporates, leaving behind pure, golden ghee.

  5. Straining & Storage

    The pure ghee is then strained to remove any remaining milk solids. It is cooled and stored in airtight containers, ready to be enjoyed in its most authentic form.

This method preserves the nutritional integrity and rich flavor that industrial processes often miss.
